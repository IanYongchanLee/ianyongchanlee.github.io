Sustainability & GHG Analytics

NYC Building Carbon Inventory &
LL97 Risk Modeling

Building a Scope 1 & 2 Carbon Inventory and Financial Penalty Simulation for Large-Scale Office Assets using NYC Open Data (LL84/LL97).

Dashboard 1: Rockefeller Portfolio Baseline

Dashboard 2: NYC Market Benchmark

01. Problem Definition

The NYC Local Law 97 (LL97) mandates strict carbon emission limits with significant financial penalties. This project leverages NYC Open Data to construct a comprehensive carbon inventory for a "Pseudo-Rockefeller Portfolio" and model potential penalty risks across different regulatory phases.

02. Methodology

Carbon Accounting & Metric Standardization

Standardized disparate fuel units into tCO₂e by applying CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O factors, scaling results to kgCO₂e/ft² for granular weighted intensity analysis.

LL97 Penalty Modeling

Engineered a simulation engine using time-bound regulatory limits to forecast annual financial liabilities based on the statutory $268/ton penalty.

03. Results & Insights

Portfolio Baseline

Rockefeller’s 2024 emissions are largely Scope 2–driven, with a few high-intensity assets driving disproportionate risk and LL97 penalty exposure escalating sharply across future compliance periods.

NYC Large Office Benchmark

Relative to NYC ≥ 200k ft² office peers, Rockefeller properties sit within the market distribution but include higher-intensity outliers, making peer-context (borough/scale) benchmarking essential for accurate interpretation.
