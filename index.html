Predicting Crop Yield with Climate Factors

Project Purpose:
Aimed to ensure food security as the global population and climate instability continue to rise. This study focuses on predicting the yields of corn, soybean, and wheat using a machine learning approach to empower data-driven agricultural policies.

Methodology:
Utilized six years of county-level data for five climate variables: temperature, precipitation, solar radiation, CO₂, and soil moisture. An XGBoost model was trained for each crop, and the SHAP framework was implemented to ensure model interpretability.

Results & Conclusion:
Achieved high predictive accuracy with R² values of 0.76 for corn and 0.81 for soybean. SHAP analysis confirmed that summer temperature and precipitation are the most influential predictors, aligning with established agronomic principles.

English Premier League Athlete Performance Model

Project Purpose:
Investigated whether a player’s age significantly impacts their on-field performance outcomes, defined as non-penalty goals and assists per 90 minutes, within the English Premier League.

Methodology:
Developed a multiple linear regression model using data from 456 players during the 2023-2024 season. To address data sparsity across nationalities, players were clustered into five continental groups, and a stepwise regression was performed to select the most efficient model based on simplicity and Mean Squared Error (MSE).

Results & Conclusion:
Out of 7 models, the best performed final model yielded an adjusted R² of 0.4644 and revealed a positive relationship between age and performance within the 17–38 age range. Multicollinearity was ruled out via VIF testing, establishing a stable baseline for comparing athlete productivity regardless of origin.

Global Life Expectancy Analysis (Tableau)

Project Purpose:
Explored the relationship between food consumption, nutrient intake, and life expectancy across continents to identify dietary patterns of long-living populations.

Methodology:
Integrated 2018–2022 data from the FAO and World Bank Group. Used Tableau to create interactive visualizations, including box plots for continental distribution and radar charts for multi-nutrient supply comparisons.

Results & Conclusion:
Found strong positive correlations between protein/energy intake and life expectancy. Recommended increasing fish and dairy consumption while ensuring sufficient daily caloric and protein intake for improved longevity.
